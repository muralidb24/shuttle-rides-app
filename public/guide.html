Neighborhood shuttle rides

A quick guide to requesting and giving rides to the airport shuttle stop.

1 Sign in

Enter your email on the sign-in screen. You'll get an email with a link — click it and you're in, no password needed. The first time, we'll ask what to call you.

2 Request a ride

Tap "Request a ride" from your dashboard. Choose whether you need a ride to the shuttle (heading out) or from the shuttle (coming home), then enter the shuttle's date and time. By default, every neighbor gets notified - see the next step if you'd rather limit that.

3 Choose who sees your requests (optional)

Open your profile menu (top right) and tap "Who sees my requests" to control the audience for every ride request you create from then on: everyone (the default), everyone except some neighbors you pick, or only some neighbors you pick. You can change this setting any time, and it applies going forward, not to requests you've already sent.

4 Turn on calendar sync (optional)

Tap "Calendar sync" on your dashboard and paste in your calendar's private feed URL, so the app can check your availability automatically and skip asking you for rides when you're already busy.

Google Calendar: Settings → pick your calendar → "Integrate calendar" → copy the "Secret address in iCal format."

Outlook / Outlook.com: Settings → Calendar → "Shared calendars" → publish a calendar and copy the ICS link.

Apple Calendar (iCloud): Right-click the calendar → Share Calendar → check "Public Calendar" → copy the link, changing webcal:// to https://.

Skip this and you'll simply be asked directly, every time a neighbor needs a ride.

5 Offer to give a ride

When a neighbor needs a ride, you'll see it under "Needs your response" on your dashboard, and get an email too. If you've turned on calendar sync, we only ask when you're free — just tap "Offer to give a ride." Otherwise you'll be asked if you're available and willing. You also won't be asked if you're already giving another ride within about two hours of that time.

6 Add it to your calendar

Once you commit to a ride, we'll offer to add it to your calendar. If you'd rather not, we'll email you a reminder the day before and the day of instead.

7 Track and cancel rides

Your dashboard has two tabs: Committed (rides you're giving) and Requested (rides you've asked for). Plans change — either side can cancel a ride any time from these lists. Unmatched ride requests also clean themselves up automatically an hour after the shuttle time.
